Chamberlin Gate

A 130-year-old scientific discipline, brought to AI reasoning.

Chamberlin Gate puts T. C. Chamberlin's method of multiple working hypotheses (1890) into an AI agent's workflow. Before the agent commits to a course, it must lay out the rival explanations, score them against real constraints, and record why the losing paths lost — so no single favoured idea rules unchecked.

The method itself is public domain and belongs to everyone. This is a small, open tool that helps an agent actually practise it. The tool is free to use; the name and its stewardship are reserved, the way a lighthouse gives its light freely but keeps the deed to the tower.
